# üìä HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard
**HR Analytics | Data Visualization | Business Insight**
This project analyzes **employee attrition** using historical employee data and presents it in an **interactive dashboard** to help HR teams and management understand attrition patterns and identify high-risk employee segments.

---

## üéØ Project Overview

Employee attrition is a significant challenge for companies because it has a direct impact on **recruitment costs**, **team stability**, and **business continuity**, especially when it occurs among productive employees and those in strategic positions.

Although HR data is usually quite comprehensive, without structured visualization and analysis, it is difficult to use this data to answer critical questions quickly and accurately.

This project focuses on:
- Presenting a comprehensive overview of attrition
- Revealing attrition patterns based on employee characteristics
- Providing relevant insights for HR policy discussions

---

## üîç Business Questions

The analysis in this project is designed to answer the following questions:

1. Which departments and job roles experience the highest attrition?
2. Which age groups are most vulnerable to leaving the company?
3. Are there differences in attrition based on gender?
4. What is the relationship between job satisfaction and attrition?
5. Which employee segments should be prioritized in retention strategies?

---

## üìä Dataset Overview

The dataset used is **historical employee data** covering active and inactive employees, with the following main coverage:

- **Demographics**: age, gender, marital status
- **Employment**: department, job role, job level, overtime
- **Compensation & benefits**: monthly income, stock options
- **Job satisfaction & engagement**
- **Work experience**: length of service, promotions, total experience
- **Attrition status**

This dataset reflects realistic HR conditions and is commonly used in workforce analytics analysis.

## üìà Key Insights Summary

The key insights gained from the analysis are:

- **The company's attrition rate is 16.12%**
- The **25‚Äì34 age group shows a relatively higher attrition rate**, especially among male employees
- The **Sales** and **R&D** departments contribute the most to attrition
- Attrition tends to be higher among **male employees** than female employees
- Employees with **low job satisfaction** show a higher tendency for attrition
- Job roles such as **Sales Executive** and **Research Scientist** have significant attrition rates

These insights show that attrition does not occur randomly, but is concentrated in certain segments.

---

## üí° Business Implications

The analysis results indicate that companies can:

- Focus retention programs on **high-risk age groups and job roles**
- Evaluate job satisfaction factors as part of HR strategy
- Use dashboards as a tool for continuous attrition monitoring
- Support data-driven HR decision-making, rather than assumptions

This dashboard is designed as an **exploration tool**, not just a static report.
